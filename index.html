About

Research across connected disciplines.

My work is centered on understanding, computing, and improving decisions in dynamic engineering systems. I move between mathematical theory, system modeling, algorithm development, numerical computation, control, and machine learning depending on the problem. Rather than being defined by one technique, my research is driven by the structure of the system and the decisions it requires.

My research has included continuous-time and discrete-time systems, dynamic optimization, optimal control, model predictive control, multiparametric methods, mathematical modeling, scientific computing, numerical algorithms, data-driven modeling, and machine learning.

A multiparametric solution is not merely a family of similar curves.

The initial state acts as a parameter. Different parts of parameter space can activate different constraints and produce different control structures, such as passing on the left, passing on the right, braking before a maneuver, or accelerating before a merge. The policy map below makes that structural change visible.

This browser experience is an illustrative control demonstration. The reference policies are lightweight computed strategies rather than certified solutions from the continuous-time multiparametric solver used in my research.
